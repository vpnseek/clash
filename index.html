The Definitive Clash Setup Guide

Whether you're looking to bypass geo-restrictions or just secure your workflow, this guide covers the "how-to" for importing your Clash subscription across all your major devices.

💻 Windows (Clash for Windows)

1. Copy Link: Grab your clash:// or HTTP subscription URL from your provider.
2. Profiles: Open the app and click the Profiles tab on the left sidebar.
3. Import: Paste your URL into the top bar and hit Download. If successful, you’ll see a new green profile card.
4. Activate: Click the card to select it, then go to General and toggle on System Proxy.

🍎 macOS (ClashX / Clash Pro)

1. Config: Click the Clash icon in your Menu Bar (top right).
2. Subscription: Navigate to Config > Remote Config > Manage.
3. Add: Click Add, paste your URL, and give it a name (e.g., "Main Sub").
4. Switch: Under the Menu Bar icon, select Proxy Mode (Rule is recommended) and ensure Set as System Proxy is checked.

📱 Android (Clash for Android)

1. Profile: Tap Profiles on the main dashboard.
2. New Profile: Tap the + icon and select URL.
3. Details: Paste your URL in the "URL" field and save. Click the "Download" icon next to the new profile.
4. Start: Return to the home screen and tap the large Stopped button to toggle it to Running.

🍏 iOS (Stash / Shadowrocket)

Note: Clash Verge/Core isn't natively on iOS; Stash is the most popular Clash-compatible client.

1. Download: Open Stash and go to the Settings tab.
2. Config: Tap Config File > Download Config from URL.
3. Paste: Enter your subscription link and tap Download.
4. Connect: Go to the home tab and toggle the switch. Allow the iOS VPN permission pop-up.

🌐 Router (OpenWrt / OpenClash)

1. Access: Log into your router’s WebUI (usually 192.168.1.1).
2. Plugin: Go to Services > OpenClash.
3. Config Update: Find Config Update or Config Subscription in the side menu.
4. Add: Paste your link into the subscription list, save, and hit Update Config.
5. Enable: Go back to the OverView page and click Enable OpenClash.
⚠️ Pro-tip: Always use "Rule" mode to keep your local traffic fast while proxying only what's necessary!
